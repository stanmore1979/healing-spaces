Healing Spaces: Transforming Homelessness

Healing Spaces is an innovative project addressing street homelessness by combining art therapy, social capital, and live–work environments. Founded on lived experience, it offers a pioneering model that supports recovery through creative expression and community.

Our Vision

The project transforms disused residential properties in central London into temporary, high-social-capital living and working spaces. These offer peppercorn rents to therapists, architects, and artists who collaborate with street homeless individuals, providing not just accommodation but therapeutic and professional engagement.

How It Works

Healing Spaces recognises that sustainable recovery depends on restoring social networks, trust, and purpose. By embedding vulnerable individuals within interdisciplinary communities, the project fosters reciprocal belonging and personal growth rather than paternalistic support.

This short-term intervention typically lasts a few months, providing holistic care and opportunities for creative and vocational development.

Our Alignment

Aligned with Camden’s strategic goals, Healing Spaces aims to innovate rough sleeping outreach and homelessness services by repurposing empty units into healing-focused live–work hubs that break cycles of repeated homelessness by addressing trauma at its core.

Collaboration & Partnership

Healing Spaces invites collaboration with local authorities, housing providers, therapists, artists, and community organisations to pilot and scale this transformative approach. Through personal experience, professional expertise, and creative practice, it strives to rebuild lives and communities from the ground up.
